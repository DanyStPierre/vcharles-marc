Voltaire Charles-Marc

Auto Dictat Plasticien

Voltaire CHARLES-MARC, born in March of 1975, has fond memories of when his cousin, Reginald Georges, used to visit him during vacation. The two would spend hours at a time drawing, a shared passion that came naturally to both. However, as they got older, they differed in their level of pursuit: Reginald decided a career in art at ENARTS, The National School for the Arts in Port-au-Prince, Haiti, while Voltaire chose to study computer science instead at INUQUA.


In 2006, while planning a family visit, Reginald thought back on the times had spent drawing with his cousin. On the spur of the moment, he decided to bring a canvas while another friend brought a can of black paint and a can of white paint. Both Reginald and his friend encouraged Voltaire to try painting for the first time. The result was immensely successful: an exquisite portrait of Voltaire and his sister in law. By 2007 Voltaire was painting larger and larger quantities of historical portraits and popular icons. Eventually, by researching the context of the individuals he painted, he began to incorporate historical references within the portraits, arriving at the collage–like style that today defines his paintings.


Voltaire’s a body of work focuses on topics and subjects related to the United States and his native homeland, Haiti. His parents having migrated to the United States in the early 80s to early 90s, Voltaire has developed and affinity for America Culture and History. Voltaire’s paintings however appeal to people of all backgrounds and origins. In addition to exhibiting his work throughout the U.S and Haiti, Voltaire regularly invites arts students to his studio that he shares with four others artists, Famous Arts Creation, where the students have the opportunity to correlate textbooks and classroom lectures with visual aids.
